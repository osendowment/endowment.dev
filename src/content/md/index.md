# Open Source Endowment

*The world's first endowment fund for open source software.*

The Open Source Endowment (OSE) provides truly **sustainable funding** for critical open source software through a community-driven endowment — modeled on the way top universities have funded themselves for centuries.

## Why an endowment?

Our world runs on open source software that is mostly built and maintained by unpaid volunteers. Over **95%** of software codebases depend on OSS, and an average application has **500+** open source dependencies. Yet most funding sources — corporate budgets, annual donations, government programs — are volatile, and can be cut overnight.

Endowments take a different approach: donations are invested as permanent principal, and only the investment returns are spent. This produces a stable cash flow that weathers economic and political volatility. OSE is the first to apply this model to open source at large.

Incidents like [Heartbleed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heartbleed), [Log4Shell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Log4Shell), and the [XZ Utils backdoor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XZ_Utils_backdoor) have made clear what happens when critical infrastructure is underfunded. OSE exists to fix the economics.

## Three principles

- **Sustainable** — a stable, long-term funding model independent of volatile corporate and personal budgets.
- **Systemic** — supports the critical-but-underfunded projects that conventional funding models miss.
- **Efficient** — a lean, digital-first, community-led organization designed for maximum impact and transparency.

## How it works

1. **Fundraising** — Individuals, companies, and foundations donate to the endowment, forming its permanent principal.
2. **Investing** — The principal is invested in a low-risk portfolio targeting ~7–8% returns. Currently managed by [Infinite Giving](https://www.infinitegiving.com/), specialists in US nonprofits.
3. **Grantmaking** — We distribute grants based on an open, data-driven [model](https://github.com/osendowment/model) co-developed with OSE Members and the community. Target format: ~$5,000 microgrants to the most critical-but-underfunded projects.
4. **Impact tracking** — Grant recipients are tracked for accountability, and the model is continuously refined.

The target annual spend rate is **5%** of the fund, matching the approach used by leading US universities. The first funding round is planned for **Q2 2026**.

## Operating principles

- **Data-driven & SMART goals** — every donation should produce measurable impact.
- **Global outlook** — the endowment supports the health of the global open source supply chain, not of specific countries, companies, or ecosystems.
- **Maximum transparency** — open data, public governance, clear accountability.
- **Inclusive governance** — the community, not corporations, shapes strategic decisions.
- **Decentralized funding** — a broad funding base makes every contribution meaningful.
- **Neutrality** — independent from political or corporate influence.
