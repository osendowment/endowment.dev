# Nominate OSS for Funding

Know an open source project that deserves support? Help us find the most critical-but-underfunded OSS. Community nominations are an important input for our model — and **self-nominations are welcome** too.

→ **Submit a nomination** at [endowment.dev/funding](https://endowment.dev/funding/) (form requires a browser for CAPTCHA).

## Eligibility

Only **independent free and open source software** is eligible. No corporate or VC-funded projects, and no projects with associated startups. See the [FAQ](/faq/) for details.

Examples of great projects we **will not** fund:

- **Kubernetes** — originated at Google, which initially owned its trademark.
- **ClickHouse** — actively commercialized by ClickHouse Inc., which owns its trademark.

A useful test is trademark ownership. If an OSS project can turn into a sustainable business, it probably should — and OSE's role is to fund the projects that can't.

## Community input

Projects are selected through an open [model](https://github.com/osendowment/model), co-developed with OSE [Members](/about/#governance). Your nominations are one of many data inputs used by the selection model — alongside downloads, dependents, security signals, bus-factor analysis, and more.

## Funding format

OSE provides **~$5,000 microgrants** to the most valuable independent OSS. Funding is dedicated to improving security and stability, or rewarding long-term maintainers.

Two mental models guide grant usage:

- **Forward-looking grants** — a specific, measurable chunk of work that de-risks the project (fixing a class of vulnerabilities, onboarding a co-maintainer).
- **Backward-looking awards** — recognizing maintainers who sustained critical projects for years without adequate support.

## Selection process

1. **Collecting inputs** — We collect nominations from the open source community, surfacing valuable projects that need support most. These inputs help OSE and its Members finalize the grant model.
2. **Scoring projects** — Nominations feed into our [Value-Risk framework](/about/#model), alongside other inputs: downloads, dependents, security, bus factor, etc. The model finds high-value, at-risk projects.
3. **Outreach & due diligence** — The OSE team contacts top-scoring projects to verify eligibility, independence, and interest. An operational review ensures funds can be received directly or via fiscal sponsors.
4. **Approval & distribution** — The Board of Directors reviews due diligence results and approves the final grant list. **The first grant distribution round is planned for Q2 2026.**
